## ğŸ’¼ Available Versions: Open Source vs. Enterprise

Mercator is available in two editions to meet the needs of all organizations: Open Source and Enterprise.

The Enterprise edition includes two advanced features **not available** in the Open Source version:

### ğŸ¨ Business Process Modeling with BPMN 2.0

The Enterprise version allows you to visually model your business processes using BPMN (Business Process Model and Notation) 2.0, the international standard for process modeling.

<div style="flex: 0 0 40%; min-width: 300px;">

</div>
<div style="flex: 1;">

**Capabilities**:

- Creation of interactive process diagrams
- Representation of tasks, operations, and business activities
- Modeling of events, branches, and connectors
- Logical groupings and workflow steps
- Export of diagrams in standard formats
- Integration with existing mapping objects

**Advantages**:

- Clear overview End-to-end processes
- Standardized workflow documentation
- Identification of optimization points
- Easier communication with business teams
- Alignment with international standards (BPMN 2.0)
</div>
</div>
This feature makes Mercator a comprehensive business and technical mapping tool, documenting both your business processes and your IT infrastructure.

### ğŸ‘¥ Granular mapper management

The Enterprise version offers an advanced access management system that allows you to precisely define who can map what within your organization.

**Features**:

- Definition of custom "mapper" roles
- Assignment of granular permissions by object type
- Delegation of mapping by domain (network, applications, infrastructure, etc.)
- Management of areas of responsibility
- Traceability of changes per mapper
- Multi-level validation workflows

**Use Cases**:

- **Network Team**: Right to map only network equipment and VLANs
- **Application Team**: Limited access to applications and application flows
- **Security Team**: Read-only overview for risk analysis
- **Business Managers**: Mapping of processes within their scope only

**Benefits**:
- Effective collaboration between teams without the risk of cross-modification
- Compliance with the principle of least privilege
- Clear allocation of documentation responsibilities
- Compliance with audit requirements (who does what)
- Scalability for large, multi-site organizations

This feature is essential for **Complex organizations** where mapping needs to be distributed across multiple teams while maintaining overall consistency.
